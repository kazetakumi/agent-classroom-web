The Ripple
No text. No label. A single drop lands on still water at the bottom of the screen — slow concentric rings expanding from a fixed point, fading as they grow. Sage is the depth beneath the surface. The ripple is proof something is down there. Every other app uses ripples as touch feedback — caused by you. This one happens without you. The source is below. Press anywhere in the zone to open.

You
Concept B
Your Turn
The column grid — but yours. Not SAGE's turn. Yours. YOU | and a waiting cursor. The conversation is already open. There is no button to press to "enter chat." You are already in it. Tap the cursor and type directly — Sage responds and the sheet rises with the conversation already begun. This removes the intermediate step entirely. The entry point is your own turn in the dialogue, waiting for you to speak.

What are you thinking?
Concept C
The Question
A single phrase breathes in from white, holds, dissolves back. "What are you thinking?" Not Sage's name. Not a label. A question directed at you — the student — from someone who is paying attention. The phrase appears every ~20 seconds: 8s to arrive, 4s to hold, 8s to leave. The silence between appearances is as designed as the phrase itself. Ma: the pause between notes is music too. Press anywhere in the zone — visible or not — to open.
