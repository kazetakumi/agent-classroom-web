1 — Pill Handle
A small centred grey pill at the very top — the standard iOS/Android bottom-sheet drag handle. No label, no arrow. The grammar is universal: grab and pull down to dismiss. No back button is needed because the gesture is the navigation. Works for any entry point since the sheet has no memory of where it came from.
Grab & pull down No label needed Universal grammar Entry-point agnostic

2 — Dismiss Strip
A dedicated tappable strip at the top holds a ↓ arrow and the label "Dismiss" — explicit about what it does and where it sends you. More verbose than the pill but removes all ambiguity. The arrow communicates direction; the label communicates intent. Good for users unfamiliar with sheet gesture patterns.
Explicit label Arrow + text Full-width tap zone No gesture required

3 — Glyph in Strip
The dismiss lives inside the top strip — a single ↓ glyph on the right, inline with the app name. No dedicated zone, no label. The top strip doubles as the dismiss area. Most minimal of the three: the glyph is the only affordance, consistent with the top-strip language already established across all screens.
Single glyph No extra zone Top strip language Most minimal
