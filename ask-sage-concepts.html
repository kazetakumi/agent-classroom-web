A · Still Water

The name in monospace, almost invisible. The entire zone is pressable — the tap affordance is implicit. Sage is present the way a name on a door is present: always there, never demanding.

Best when you want Sage to feel like an ambient intelligence rather than a feature. The user discovers it through familiarity, not through a label.

B · The Signet

A single physical object. The circle has weight and identity — it marks Sage as a character, not a function. The "S" in Cormorant italic is the only glyph, and it carries the full personality of the app.

Best when Sage should feel like a person or persona — something you summon. The most distinct from generic AI chat UI patterns. The circle persists identically across every screen.

C · The Invitation

Sage's own words — lowercase, italic — as the component itself. The pill handle teaches the gesture before the user presses. The gradient rule softens the boundary so the component feels attached to the screen, not bolted on.

Best when the voice of Sage matters most. The text can stay fixed ("ask sage") or shift per screen context ("I'm here" on welcome, "ask me" on question). Lowest friction — the interaction is legible immediately.
